Genetic Engineering Appraisal Committee (GEAC)

What is GEAC?

The Genetic Engineering Appraisal Committee (GEAC) is a regulatory body in India responsible for the assessment and approval of activities involving the large-scale use of hazardous microorganisms and recombinants in research and industrial production. It also oversees the release of genetically engineered organisms (GMOs) and products into the environment. GEAC operates under the Ministry of Environment, Forest and Climate Change (MoEFCC) and is mandated by the Environmental Protection Act, 1986.

Mission and Objectives of GEAC

Mission

To ensure the safe and responsible use of biotechnology in India by regulating and overseeing activities involving genetically engineered organisms and hazardous microorganisms, thereby protecting human health and the environment.

Objectives

  • Regulate GMOs
  • Ensure Biosafety
  • Risk Assessment
  • Compliance and Monitoring
  • Promote Transparency
  • Capacity Building
  • Policy Development
  • Increase Public Awareness
  • Support Scientific Research

Regulatory Framework & Functions

Legal Mandate & Jurisdiction

Legislative Authority

Operates under the Environmental Protection Act, 1986, providing the legal framework for regulating GMOs and hazardous microorganisms.

Scope of Jurisdiction

Oversees activities involving large-scale use and release of genetically engineered organisms across research, industrial, and environmental sectors.

Enforcement Powers

Empowered to develop, implement, and enforce biosafety guidelines and protocols to ensure environmental and human health safety.

Regulatory Process for GMOs

Application Review

Thorough evaluation of proposals for the release, import, and commercial use of GMOs to ensure they meet safety standards.

Risk Assessment

Conducts comprehensive risk assessments to evaluate potential impacts on the environment and human health.

Decision Making

Provides approvals, rejections, or recommendations for modifications based on scientific evidence and safety considerations.

Functions and Responsibilities

Approval of GMOs

Reviews and approves applications for the release and use of genetically modified organisms in various sectors.

Biosafety Monitoring

Ensures compliance with biosafety regulations through regular monitoring and inspections.

Public Engagement

Engages with stakeholders and the public to address concerns and provide transparency in its regulatory activities.

Guidelines and Protocols

Biosafety Guidelines

Comprehensive guidelines for the safe handling, use, and disposal of GMOs and hazardous microorganisms.

Standard Operating Procedures

Established protocols for conducting risk assessments, field trials, and environmental impact assessments.

Compliance Requirements

Detailed compliance requirements for organizations and researchers working with GMOs to ensure adherence to safety standards.
